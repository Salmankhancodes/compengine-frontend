About Comp-Engine

Welcome to Comp-Engine Time Series, a comparison engine for time-series data! This website allows you to upload time-series data and interactively visualize how your data relates to time series that others have measured or generated.

How it works:

When you upload new time-series data, our servers compute properties (or ‘features’) of the data, and use these features to find similar types of data that are already in our database. You can then interactively explore how your data is placed in this broader context—maybe you’ll find a new connection or get a new idea ?!

Objectives:

We want CompEngine Time Series to be a place where you can contribute to a self-organizing, living library of time-series data. Our main objectives are twofold:

1. Tie together diverse disciplines
Time-series data are generated across the sciences and industry: heart rates measured during surgery , chaotic laser bursts measured in physics laboratories , sensors on industrial production lines , vast records of credit card transactions , archaeological timelines , and medieval music scores . All of these data can be represented as time series, and yet they are seldom collected and analyzed together. By uploading your own data to CompEngine, you can learn about similar data measured and analyzed in other parts of science and industry. It can directly connect you to others who have uploaded similar data from another area and even help identify appropriate classes of mathematical models for your data. We hope that doing so will enable a discipline-fluid view of time-series analysis that might spark exciting new discussion and collaboration.

2. Design useful methods for time-series data
In order to design useful methods for time-series data, we first need to survey the types of time-series that we measure from the world, and understand which methods work best on which types of data. We make all uploaded data available for anyone to download and study, in the hope that this will stimulate a new generation of time-series analysis algorithms that are properly tailored to the diversity of real data.
